# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

ios

## Users

Adult returning collectors: people who collected Pokémon cards as kids and are back in
the hobby with disposable income and a lot of nostalgia. They catalog at home in the
evening and check prices standing in card shops. The job to be done is knowing what
they own, what it's worth, and what to hunt next — without ceremony. Secondary use
leans toward the same person's trading side (price history, grading ROI), served by the
same screens rather than a separate audience.

## Product Purpose

A free, open source iOS tracker for Pokémon TCG collections: on-device card scanning,
grouping, market prices with history, graded/condition pricing, and portfolio value.
Success is a collector trusting it as the permanent home of their collection — daily
check-ins, full catalogs imported, and beta testers converting to advocates — while the
app stays free, private, and community funded.

## Positioning

Everything works, free, forever. The collector-grade tracker with no paywalls: the
scanner, price history, and export all just work. Every screen should quietly reinforce
that nothing here is metered.

## Brand Personality

Clean, capable, quiet. A precision instrument that gets out of the way of the cards.
Warmth comes from the collection itself — card art, the tin glyph, small moments like
the tin loading animation — not from decorated chrome. Voice is plain and confident;
it never sells, because there is nothing to sell.

## Anti-references

- Paywall-tracker vibes (Collectr / TCGplayer-style upsells): no locked features, no
  "PRO" badges, no banners — nothing that even resembles a meter.
- Finance-app coldness (Robinhood-style charts-first austerity): prices serve the
  collection; the collection is love, not just a portfolio.
- Web-app-in-a-wrapper: no custom nav bars, hamburger menus, or web-shaped controls —
  it must feel like a real iOS app everywhere.
- Kiddie Pokémon kitsch: no Pikachu-yellow, no cartoon fonts. Respect the IP without
  cosplaying it.

## Design Principles

- Free means whole — no screen may imply a gate, meter, or tier; generosity is visible
  in the design, not announced by it.
- The cards are the hero — card art and collection data carry the emotion; chrome stays
  neutral and quiet.
- Platform-native first — HIG structure and system controls everywhere; the tool
  disappears into the collecting task.
- Trust through quiet precision — prices, history, and stats presented plainly and
  legibly, never dramatized.
- One brand moment at a time — the tin glyph and its animation are the identity;
  deploy them at loading and empty moments, not as decoration on every screen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Solid iOS platform defaults as the floor: Dynamic Type throughout, VoiceOver labels on
interactive and image content, Reduce Motion honored (already done in the tin loading
animation), and readable contrast in both light and dark appearance.
